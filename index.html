1. Nuremberg Trials

What was the primary purpose of the Nuremberg Trials held after World War II?

  • A. To negotiate the surrender of Germany
  • B. To prosecute Nazi leaders for war crimes
  • C. To divide Germany into Allied occupation zones
  • D. To establish the United Nations
Correct Answer: B
Verification: The Nuremberg Trials (1945–1946) prosecuted senior Nazi leaders for war crimes and crimes against humanity.

2. Australian Coat of Arms

Why do Australia’s coat of arms feature a kangaroo and an emu?

  • A. They were the first animals recorded by Captain Cook
  • B. They are the largest animals native to Australia
  • C. They cannot easily walk backwards
  • D. They symbolise Australia's two largest states
Correct Answer: C
Verification: The kangaroo and emu are commonly interpreted as symbols of national progress because both animals rarely move backwards.

4. Which Is NOT a Country
